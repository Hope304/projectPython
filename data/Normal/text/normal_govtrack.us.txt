Even though they have parts of the government funding split by date, everyone has treated the first date as the one that matters. So, if no real appropriations are passed between now and, say, Tuesday, they'll do this again next week. Reauthorizing the FAA The House passed another short term extension of existing authorization on 2/29 by 401-19. The Senate has until next Friday, 3/8 to pass it. Small Business H.R. 7102: Native American Entrepreneurial Opportunity Act passed on 2/29 by 402-16. This bill would establish an Office of Native American Affairs within the Small Business Administration. Redevelopment H.R. 4984: D.C. Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus Revitalization Act passed on 2/28 by 348-55. This bill transfers authority for redevelopment of the old RFK Stadium to Washington, D.C. Nuclear Energy H.R. 6544: Atomic Energy Advancement Act passed on 2/28 by 365-36. According to the Hill, the bill would speed up environmental reviews, reduce fees for new reactors and extend existing liability protections for nuclear accidents. Failed Veto Override In January, the House passed a disapproval of a waiver on buy America requirements for electric vehicle chargers that had passed the Senate last November. President Biden quickly vetoed it and on 2/29 the Senate attempted a veto override which failed 50-47.
